SECTION 1

Customer: Good morning, madam. Can I help you?
Customer: Yes. Hello. I have a complaint to make about this vacuum cleaner. I’m wanting to return it, please.
Sales assistant: Oh, dear. I’m sorry to hear that. Well, could I take down your details first?
Sales assistant: Your name, please.
Customer: It’s Diane Ruddick. That’s R‑U‑D‑D‑I‑C‑K.
Sales assistant: Fine. Thanks. And your address?
Customer: It’s 34 Garden Avenue.
Sales assistant: I haven’t heard of that one. That’s here in Hamilton, is it?
Customer: Yes. It is. It’s just a small street.
Sales assistant: Right. And what number can I reach you on?
Customer: That would be 970‑7520, and that’s home because my mobile is not working at the moment. And at the office, I share a phone line, so it’s better not to call me at work.
Sales assistant: Okay then. And now a few details about the product itself. When did you buy it? Do you remember?
Customer: Yes. It says on the receipt… hang on. I need my glasses to read it. Does that say July 21? Oh, no. Sorry. It’s the 31st July.
Sales assistant: And I see you’ve got the vacuum with you. It looks like a Volta budget model. What’s the size of the motor on that one?
Customer: It’s 104 watts, I think.
Sales assistant: Right. And what exactly is the problem?
Customer: The handle is broken. I can’t push it properly.
Sales assistant: I see. Do you want a replacement or a …?
Customer: I’d like a repair, please. Also, it didn’t come with a dust bag.
Sales assistant: That’s strange. Let me check the receipt. And the price?
Customer: 180 dollars.
Sales assistant: And what colour is it?
Customer: Silver.
Sales assistant: All right, I’ve noted everything. We’ll process your complaint.
