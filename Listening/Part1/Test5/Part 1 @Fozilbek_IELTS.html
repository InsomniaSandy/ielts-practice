Transcription

SECTION 1

Part one. You hear a conversation about recycling and garbage collection services.

Caller: Hello. I recently moved into a new house in Avondale, and I’m trying to get a handle on the garbage collection and recycling services. Could you help me with that?
Clerk: Certainly. We’ve recently adjusted our schedule to better meet community needs. While our collection in Avondale used to be on Thursdays, we’ve shifted to Tuesdays due to popular demand. We recommend setting out your garbage at the designated collection sites the night before to ensure timely pickup.

Caller: That’s helpful. Thanks. What time should I expect the collection truck on that date?
Clerk: Our trucks are scheduled to arrive and begin collection at 08:15 a.m. This timing helps avoid the rush hour, usually between 06:30 a.m. and 07:30 a.m., and ensures that the roads are less congested.

Caller: That sounds well planned. I have an old TV that I need to dispose of. Do you recycle electronics?
Clerk: Currently, we do not have the facilities to safely recycle electronic appliances like TVs. These items require special handling due to their components and materials. I recommend checking with local electronic stores or municipal waste facilities. They often offer electronics recycling programs.

Caller: Okay. I understand. Do you offer door-to-door garbage collection services?
Clerk: Unfortunately, due to our current staffing challenges, we cannot provide door-to-door service. However, all major garbage collection sites are clearly marked on the map available on our website. This should help you locate the nearest disposal point conveniently.

Caller: Great. Where can I get the special bags for sorting waste?
Clerk: Bags for sorting waste can be bought in grocery stores. And any tips for recycling? Divide waste into different categories. Remove labels of bottles before disposal. Empty water from juice bottles. Remove food inside pizza boxes. Separate egg cartons from other waste.
