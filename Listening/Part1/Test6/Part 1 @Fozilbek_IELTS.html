SECTION 1

Questions 1–10

[Manager] Hello. Greenwell Garden Centers.
[Caller] Oh, hello. I’m calling about the part-time job you were advertising last week.
[Manager] Oh, you mean for the delivery driver?
[Caller] No. For a general assistant. My son’s looking for a part-time job, and I said I’d call you about it. I already picked up an application form from one of your staff.

[Manager] I see. Well, we’ll shortly have a position available in our new Market Road branch that’s opening next month, or there’s one available right now in our branch in Station Road, and that’s where the actual job advert was for.
[Caller] It’s definitely the current job I’m calling about.
[Manager] Right. Now there were a couple of options regarding working hours. We’re really looking for someone that could do both Monday and Wednesday afternoons, or the same hours on Thursday and Friday.
[Caller] What about Sunday? That’d work, provided he could do a full day.
[Manager] That wouldn’t be a problem.
[Caller] Great. And what would the job involve exactly?
[Manager] Let me just look at the job description for that particular job. Well, looks like there’d be some working on the checkout, you know, taking payment for goods, and I suppose our general assistants also do rather a lot of lifting of some quite heavy items. So good physical strength is quite important for this position. There’s some assisting with customer inquiries as well. But we don’t expect new staff to have much knowledge about the plants themselves. That’s what the more experienced staff are there for. Then, especially in the summer, the plants need a lot of watering.
[Caller] I see. Well, I think you could manage all that.
[Manager] Good. Now there are a few other qualifications and skills that the job calls for. The first one being communication skills are important. As you know, first impressions count for a lot. And the other thing we would be looking for in our applicants for this position is he should have some ability in maths, a basic level at least.
[Caller] Well, he did well in that subject at school, and he’s polite and sociable too. So, hopefully, he’ll match your expectations.
[Manager] Great.

[Caller] So am I right in thinking he’s still at high school?
[Manager] He’s about to finish for the summer break, so he could come for an interview this week if that’s convenient.
[Caller] How about Wednesday, say about 10AM?
[Manager] I’m afraid that’s the morning of his last exam. Could we make it later instead, perhaps at 02:35?
[Caller] Five would be better for us.
[Manager] Okay. That’s fine. So his name is?
[Caller] It’s Liam Avery.
[Manager] Okay. Got that. So Liam will be having the interview with me. My name’s Maria Rapana, r a p for Peter, a n a. On Tuesday, Liam needs to ring me on my direct line to confirm that he’s coming, and that’s 0213033874. Okay. Oh, just one more thing. I need to see Liam’s passport at the interview just to see that he’s eligible to work in this country.
[Caller] Sure. I’ll tell him. Thanks for your time.
[Manager] Thanks for calling.

That is the end of section one.
