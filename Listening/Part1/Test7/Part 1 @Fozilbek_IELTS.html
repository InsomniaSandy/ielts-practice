SECTION 1

Part one. You will hear a reporter from a community newspaper phoning a woman who is organizing a local fair.

Reporter: Good morning. Is that Miss Flint?
Miss Flint: Yes. Speaking.
Reporter: It’s Rick Graham here from the community newspaper. We want to print an article about the local fair to encourage more people to come. Could I ask you a few questions about the event?
Miss Flint: Yes. Of course.
Reporter: So the fair’s on Saturday, isn’t it? And I know that every year the fair raises money for something local.
Miss Flint: Yes, that’s right. Last year we were able to help the school, and this time, after careful thought, we’ve decided to support the hospital because that’s obviously important.
Reporter: Okay. I’ll make a note of that. And then in terms of attendance, how many people do you think will come?
Miss Flint: Well, it depends a bit on the weather. If it rains for twenty-four hours before the event, it does put some people off. But in the past, we’ve had approximately 3,000 visitors, and I expect it’ll be roughly the same this time.
Reporter: So that’s why you need a big venue. It’s at Battersby Park. Right?
Miss Flint: Yes. It’s the same place every year.
Reporter: I’m actually new to the area. Could you spell that for me?
Miss Flint: Sure. It’s Battersby, B A T T E R S B Y.
Reporter: Great. Thanks. Okay. So if my readers come to the fair, what sort of activities can they do?
Miss Flint: Well, we’ve got lots of things. One example is what we’re calling the swap shop. Children can bring in a toy from home that they don’t want anymore and exchange it for something else. That’s a good idea. Then in the afternoon there’s going to be a basketball competition. That’s mixed teams, boys and girls. After that, we’ll be doing the same thing again, but this time it’ll be soccer. Unfortunately, the cricket pitch is closed for maintenance at the moment.
Reporter: I’ll mention that in the article. And then anything else, what can I tell people that’ll really make them want to come to the fair?
Miss Flint: Well, for the first time this year, we’ll have some classic vehicles for you to ride in. One of the highlights is you’ll be able to ride right around the park in an old ambulance. It was built in the 1950s, so it’s a chance to experience how they did things in the past. That should be quite exciting, with the lights flashing and bells going.
Reporter: Exactly. Then we’ve also got an international visitor coming, Kiko Mahara, who’s going to be running a workshop on making models.
Miss Flint: Oh, I’ve heard of her. She uses paper to create the most amazing little animals, planes, all sorts of things.
Reporter: That’s right. I think it’ll be a lot of fun.
