SECTION 2

Questions 11–15

My name’s Janice Martin, and I’m here to tell you about the relaxation programs we are going to offer staff at your company and what the benefits might be for you. First, a bit of background about who we are and what we offer. Many companies, large and small, are now offering relaxation programs for staff at all levels. Our first client was Cooper’s, the multinational finance company. To begin with, we were called in by the chief executive to run programs for directors who were dealing with a particularly stressful time for the business, originally only for senior management, and it proved so popular that sometime later, we started running classes open to all employees. I expect you’ve all heard of Audiovac. With this company, it wasn’t a decision from the top that resulted in running relaxation classes, but a few admin staff, mostly women, who thought it would be a good idea. requested by staff. I understand that some of them have found it so beneficial that they’ve organized relaxation classes in their children’s schools. A different option, which we’ve been running for Duke hotels, is relaxation weekends. These are held at one of their hotels every month. The great thing about this scheme is that staff can bring their husbands or wives and children. monthly breaks for employees and families. Duke Hotels also offer this service for business clients who want to combine relaxation with training weekends for their staff at all levels in the company. We also run a range of courses for staff at First Bank. There are relaxation only groups, which are attended mainly by people in their forties and fifties, most popular with 40+ age group, and more energetic yoga sessions for the physically fit. We’re also considering holding yoga sessions, which we’ll make sure appeal to both male and female members of staff. You may be surprised to hear that Carlton Football Club is another of our clients. We’ve had a contract to provide weekly sessions for the team for the last four years, started four years ago, and we’ve developed special techniques which the coaches can build into the footballer’s daily training regime. They seem to find this really helpful.

Questions 16–20

Now I’m sure you’re all aware that relaxation practice, which usually includes both meditation and yoga, helps with stress reduction. But there are lots of other benefits too. For example, it’s been found that it leads to an increase in the productivity of managers. The majority find they now focus on one thing at a time and don’t allow themselves to get distracted by emails or phone calls if they’re in a meeting working on a team project. they concentrate better. We’ve also found that managers get better at making decisions as a direct result of learning how to control the mind during relaxation exercises. And this improves with practice. As people get better at meditating, they’re able to see things from other people’s point of view more easily, and so benefit from advice they might have ignored before when taking important decisions. One thing I’ve been frequently told by people I train is that relationships with colleagues who are also doing relaxation classes improve over time. I don’t know if that’s got anything to do with becoming a calmer, less aggressive person, but what I do know is that if people have got something more to talk about than just work, real friendships can develop no matter what job people are doing. So I hope I’ve persuaded you to come and try classes which start next week. To start with, we’ll be doing sessions on Tuesdays, repeated on Fridays, both during the lunch break, and depending how it goes, we may introduce after work sessions later in the year. If large numbers of people are interested, we may eventually need to increase the number of sessions further as we avoid having too many in a group. Each session will contain both meditation and a bit of gentle yoga, nothing too energetic. You won’t need to buy any special equipment for these sessions. The mats will be provided, and we’ll be doing a lot of exercises sitting on chairs. You won’t need to wear special sports clothes, but as your body temperature drops during meditation, you should wear something warm.
