PART 2

Radio announcer: Good morning, everyone. This is Kevin Lai on National Radio, and today I’m going to tell you what’s so special about the laid-back city of Gisborne. You’ll hear a little bit about its past and a lot about the attractions it has to offer both foreign and domestic visitors today.

The Maori name for the Gisborne region is Tūranganui-a-Kiwa, which means “the east coast”. Early European settlers called it Poverty Bay because they found it difficult to grow crops there, but that name didn’t last long. The first European to land here was Captain James Cook in 1769.

In the early days, Gisborne’s main exports came from its farms — wool, meat, and dairy products. These days, the region is famous for its citrus fruits. It actually exports large quantities of oranges and lemons to Asia every year. There are also some excellent wineries producing both red and white grapes.

If you’re visiting in summer, don’t miss the Gisborne Summer Concert. It takes place in one of the local vineyards and features top New Zealand musicians. It’s a fantastic evening under the stars.

Even if it rains — and it does rain quite a bit in Gisborne — there’s still plenty to do. The announcer recommends a cultural display in the museum. There’s currently an excellent exhibition called “The Beach” with beautiful photographs and traditional Maori artefacts.

Gisborne also has a number of attractions that are especially suitable for different groups of visitors.

If you’re a recently married couple looking for a romantic spot, head to the Hot Springs Reserve. The warm pools are perfect for a relaxing soak.

For young adults, particularly university students or backpackers, the Mahia Peninsula is ideal. There are great surfing spots and a lively nightlife scene.

Families with young school children will love the Motu River Rafting experience. It’s safe, exciting, and the guides are excellent with kids.

Finally, for disabled people, Eden Woodlands Park is highly recommended. It has excellent wheelchair access, wide paths, and beautiful gardens that are easy to explore.

That’s all for today. I hope you enjoy your visit to Gisborne!

That is the end of part two.
