SECTION 2

Good afternoon, everyone, and welcome to my talk about the Hamilton car sale, one of the largest community events of its kind in the region. I'd like to begin with a few general points before we move on to the layout of the sale grounds. In earlier years, the Hamilton car sale attracted around nine fifty people each week, but that figure belongs to the past. At present, the number has grown to approximately 1,500 visitors weekly, showing how popular the event has become. In fact, if the trend continues, we expect attendance to rise by another 500 people next year.

Now turning to the matter of fees. The entry fee varies depending on the type of vehicle with certain categories charged at a higher rate. Of course, there is also a separate fee for sellers, which is set differently from what buyers pay, so it's not the same for both groups. And while some people assume the cost somehow links back to whether or not the vehicle is successfully sold, that's not the case here.

Another important rule concerns information displayed on the vehicles. Every vehicle that comes onto the grounds is expected to display the proper information. Some people think it's enough to put a sticker showing the price, but that doesn't meet the regulations. Others simply attach a for sale sign, yet that alone isn't acceptable either. What's actually required is a full details sheet placed on the vehicle so buyers can see all the essential information at a glance.

Alongside that, you'll also hear about the information report. Visitors sometimes assume this is mainly about the status of the vehicle, but that isn't what it's for. Its real purpose is to confirm that the car is genuinely owned by the person offering it for sale, giving buyers peace of mind. Some also believe it lists how many previous owners a vehicle has had, yet that's not part of the document.

Finally, a word of advice for anyone completing a sale. The main piece of guidance we give is that once you've successfully sold your vehicle, you should take steps right away to record the change of ownership so the legal responsibility clearly passes to the new driver. We strongly advise people not to accompany a buyer on a test drive since that can raise issues of liability. And while some assume the key point is about collecting the money, that isn't the official emphasis here.

Before you hear the rest of the talk, you have some time to look at questions 16 to 20.

Now listen and answer questions 16 to 20.

Let me now describe the layout of the sale areas so you can find your way around the grounds. To reach the section reserved for cars priced under $5,000, start at the main entrance and walk directly north along the central path. After reaching the end of this street, you'll need to make a left turn. Keep following this route, and as you go, pay attention to the surroundings. Just before you come to the trees area, you'll notice the lot for budget cars clearly marked on your right hand side. That's the place where all vehicles under $5,000 are displayed.

To find the section for cars in the 5 to $10,000 range, begin once again at the main entrance and head north along the main pathway. Keep walking until you notice an office building on your left hand side, which is the point where you need to turn left. From there, simply continue straight ahead in the same direction. As you approach the river at the edge of the grounds, the area for vehicles in this price range will come into view right in front of you.

To reach the display area for cars priced over $10,000 start once again from the main entrance and walk straight ahead along the central route. Continue on this path until you come across the office building directly ahead of you. Opposite the office, you'll notice a short side road leading away from the main path. Take this road, and follow it straight through to the very end, where the section for higher priced vehicles is clearly set out.

To locate the section for caravans, look carefully towards the northwest corner of the grounds. It's positioned just beneath the bridge that crosses New North Road, making it very easy to identify. This spot is also right next to the north gate, so if you approach from there, you'll find the caravan's area almost immediately.

To find the section set aside for motorbikes, begin at the main entrance and head directly north, continuing straight all the way to the end of the central path. When you reach the far side, make a right turn. As you follow this road, keep an eye to your right hand side, because the motorbike area is located partway along, right in the middle of that stretch of road.

That brings us to the end of the overview. I hope this guide will help you navigate the Hamilton car sale more easily and make the most of your visit.

That is the end of part two. You now have thirty seconds to check your answers to part two.
