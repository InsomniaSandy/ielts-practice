SECTION 3

Questions 21-25

Hi, Ben. I have so much to tell you.
Me too.
How did your internship go? It was brilliant. I worked in a big department store. I thought you were going into your family business.
I decided I'd learn more in a company where I wasn't the boss's daughter, so I went to this store in my hometown. When I was in high school, I was a sales assistant there for a couple of summers, and I still knew some people there, so it was easy to set up. Our professor recommended working in a manufacturing company, and I contacted a couple of firms, but it came to nothing.
And did you learn much that helped with our classes?
Sure. There was a big problem while I was working there. Do you remember a massive advertising campaign for a new brand of sunglasses a few weeks ago?
Yeah.
Well, sales rocketed and we ran out. Customers started complaining and I was asked to track down a supply. I had to call around the manufacturers and wholesalers until I could find a batch. I managed to find a local one, then we had to pay them up front as they weren't our usual supplier. But my boss was pretty pleased with me. We were only out of stock for a few hours in the end.
Great. So what was the best thing about your internship?
For one thing, my coworkers were very helpful. I learned a lot from them.
Mine were friendly enough, but I wouldn't say I picked up much from them. The only good thing about it was having to deal with issues that came up. Classes just don't prepare you for a lot of them.
Yeah, that's true.
Like the fact that sometimes you have to do two or three things at the same time because they're all urgent.
That's right.
Anyway, how was your internship, Ben? You were with a garden furniture manufacturer, weren't you?
That's right. My experience wasn't very good though. When I had an interview there a few months ago, they said I'd spend time in every section and sit in on meetings with clients, but hardly any of that happened. I was expected to spend most of my time filing papers and getting coffee for the managers. I was really annoyed. It wasn't even as though everyone was too busy to get their own coffee.
That's awful.
Yeah. Still, I got on okay with the other people there, so it wasn't as bad as it might have been.
I think the college should make sure students are placed in suitable companies for internships. Don't you?
Well, I like the current system where we arrange the internship ourselves, and then the college comes in and formalizes the arrangement. But they need to spell out the purpose of the internship and send the companies a list of their responsibilities. Too many businesses just think of us as free labor.
Yes. And it would be helpful if we had a couple of classes where we can learn about the company.
Oh, I think it's easy enough to research that on the Internet.
Maybe.

Questions 26-30

Are you going to do anything about your internship, Ben? You wouldn't want anyone going to the same company next year and having a similar experience, would you?
No. Were you the first intern they had from this college?
No. There's someone in the year above us who was there. I should have asked her about it before I went.
Well, I think the first step is to talk to her (previous intern). Then you need to make some notes and discuss the problem with the professor. He can advise you whether or not to take it any further.
If I decide to take it further, I think I should then contact the company itself and find out if they have any feedback about my internship.
Yes. There may be a valid reason why it was so poor.
Maybe it makes more sense to write to the chief executive.
I wouldn't make it too formal at this stage. If the college makes a complaint, they'll write to the chief executive. I don't think you should.
Okay. So the person I had most contact with, my line manager?
That would be better. But if you're still not satisfied, contact whoever it is in the college who's in charge of our internships. It's the head of the department, isn't it?
It was, but the college appointed an internship program organizer last fall. I've got their name on some paperwork somewhere.
One other thing you might want to do after that is check the law on internships.
I'll talk to one of the college's student advisers in the department. She takes one of the same classes as me.
Okay. Let's hope there isn't any trouble with that.
