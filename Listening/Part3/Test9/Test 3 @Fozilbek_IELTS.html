Morning Olive, I hear you wanted some feedback on your assignment?
That’s right. I’ve analysed different media platforms for advertising and pulled out some key points.
Great, let’s start. For the introduction, I’m planning to highlight one main advantage of each medium. Like with trade publications, the big plus is you’re reaching your target audience — people already interested in the industry.
Exactly, that’s why they’re such a popular choice in the business community. So did you look at email advertising?
Yep, it’s got lots of benefits. But the standout for me is how direct it is. You can send your message straight to people without going through any middlemen.
True, another bonus is you can give real-time updates with the latest information.
Yeah, but I didn’t really want to focus on that, because loads of media channels can do that now.
Fair enough. What about general-interest magazines?
How do you see them? Well, their biggest strength is they last longer than most other print media. Unlike newspapers that get tossed quickly, magazines hang around because of their high production quality, glossy pages, nice layouts, and things like that.
Good point. Not the greenest option, but they often get passed on rather than binned right away. Moving on to websites.
The advantages are pretty obvious. They make a business look modern and professional, but what I really want to stress is that a website gives you access to international markets.
Exactly, basically no limits at all. Then there’s cinema advertising.
The downside is the audience is too broad — you can’t really target a specific group. But the huge plus is people can’t skip it — they have to sit through the ad.
Absolutely, they can’t just click away or walk out easily. I also thought about trade fairs,

Okay, what angles are you going to cover and why?
I’ll start with budgeting. I’ve found some good tips for planning TV ad budgets. Some are obvious, like it’s way cheaper if the company can produce the ad in-house.
