SECTION 4

Questions 31-40

[Lecturer] Today, I'd like to talk about one of the world's most fascinating archaeological mysteries, the Nazca Lines in Peru. These enormous designs carved into the desert ground have puzzled scientists and visitors alike for decades. Let's take a closer look at their location and construction, then move on to their purpose and theories, and finally explore recent discoveries and threats to their preservation.

When we talk about where these lines were made, they actually lie deep in a remote desert area in Southern Peru. The climate there is extremely dry and hot, which helped the drawings survive for thousands of years. So instead of being in a forest or valley, they're spread across this vast sandy landscape.

Archaeologists discovered that the ancient builders didn't have any advanced machines. They relied on very basic tools to create these enormous figures. Things like wooden stakes and ropes were enough for them to mark out straight paths over the ground. It's fascinating how such primitive equipment could lead to such precise designs.

To make the drawings as accurate as possible, the Nazca people carefully set up wooden pegs across the land. They then pulled long ropes tight between those posts to mark out perfectly straight paths over the desert surface. It's amazing that such simple techniques could produce geometric patterns visible even from the sky.

Instead of using metal wires or modern measuring tapes, they mainly relied on natural cotton ropes. These natural materials allowed them to take exact measurements without the rope breaking or stretching too much. The consistency of these ropes was the secret behind the symmetry of many Nazca figures.

The surface of the land was originally covered with dark stones that had been burnt by the sun. The artist cleared those rocks away to expose the lighter coloured soil hidden below. This colour contrast made the lines stand out sharply, creating shapes that could be seen clearly from above.

Now moving on to their possible meanings and functions. Many of the giant animal drawings like the hummingbird or the monkey weren't just decorative art. Local people believed these creatures had spiritual powers connected with medicine and traditional healing. During ancient ceremonies, they probably used these animal shapes as sacred spaces for prayer or offerings to cure illness and protect their community.

However, not all experts agree with that spiritual explanation. One leading theory suggests that the lines had a practical use rather than a religious one. Some researchers think they were part of an irrigation system, basically channels designed to stop floodwaters from ruining nearby crops. The layout of these lines seems to follow the paths of underground water, suggesting they serve to control heavy rain or runoff.

Another reason the Nazca lines are still visible today is the area's unique environment. Because the region barely receives any rain, the designs have remained visible for centuries. The heat and dryness help to preserve the dark outer coating caused by dead insects and oxidised stones. Without that extreme arid environment, the drawings would probably have faded or disappeared long ago.

Let's finish with what's been happening more recently. In recent years, researchers have started using drones to scan the vast desert and have discovered several new figures, including a rare portrait of a human face.

However, the lines now face a new threat. They are increasingly affected by tourism as more and more visitors come to see them.
